You can spot an AI-generated website in seconds. Not because it is ugly. Because it is exactly like the others. That is **slop**. A page that is clean, competent, technically fine, and interchangeable with thousands of others. To make it concrete, we audited a real site: [dnaexplore.ai](https://dnaexplore.ai).

Good news: two open source projects take the problem from both ends. [Taste Skill](https://www.tasteskill.dev/) gives your coding agent design rules before it generates anything. [Impeccable](https://impeccable.style/slop/) catalogs 46 slop patterns and detects them on an existing page.

Here are the eight signs, from the most frequent to the most discreet.

## 1. The em dash, everywhere.

**What AI does:** it scatters the em dash, the `—`. In headlines, in buttons, in testimonials. It is signature number one. On the audited page it shows up **thirty one times**.

![An em dash inside a button label.](/blog/slop-emdash-button.webp)

Further down, the same character separates two amounts, where a plain hyphen would have done the job:

![An em dash used to separate two prices.](/blog/slop-emdash-price.webp)

**What you should do:** a period, a comma, a colon, or a hyphen. Never the em dash.

## 2. The eyebrow, that little label above every heading.

**What AI does:** a tiny uppercase letter-spaced label sitting immediately above an oversized headline. Or the same shape rendered as a pill chip. It is called the _eyebrow_.

![An eyebrow in spaced uppercase, inside a pill, above the page headline.](/blog/slop-eyebrow-pill.webp)

**What you should do:** drop the eyebrow. If the word really matters, fold it into the headline.

## 3. The gradient headline.

**What AI does:** a gradient on the main headline, often looping forever. Purple, blue, teal: the hue changes, the gesture does not.

![The site headline with an animated teal gradient.](/blog/slop-hero-gradient.webp)

**What you should do:** pick one accent color and hold it across the whole page. Set the headline in a single flat tone. Save the contrast for what is meant to be clicked.

## 4. The oversized italic serif headline.

**What AI does:** an oversized italic serif as the primary hero headline. On its own it reads as taste. Repeated everywhere, it has become the universal AI startup landing page hero.

![The closing headline set in Playfair Display.](/blog/slop-serif.webp)

**What you should do:** use one or two typefaces, no more. Go with a sans serif.

## 5. No real images.

**What AI does:** it writes text where an image belongs.

![The founder section, with a colored square of initials instead of a photo.](/blog/slop-founder.webp)

Sourcing an image takes a choice, a decision. That is exactly why it is missing.

**What you should do:** at least one real image on every page that matters. A product photo, a screenshot of the interface, a face. If you have nothing to show, the page is not finished.

## 6. Decorative numbering.

**What AI does:** it numbers everything. `01`, `02`, `03` above three steps already in reading order, in a section already titled "Three steps".

![Three steps labeled 01, 02, 03 under a heading that already says three steps.](/blog/slop-steps.webp)

**What you should do:** let the content carry the order. The verb is enough: "Upload", "Preview", "Unlock". A reader can count to three without help.

## 7. Identical cards.

**What AI does:** six identical cards. Same size, same white fill, same icon chip, same bold label, same grey paragraph.

![Six identical white cards in a three by two grid.](/blog/slop-cards.webp)

**What you should do:** change the layout from one section to the next.

## 8. Numbers and names that ring false.

**What AI does:** numbers with no source, and people with no face.

![A bar of four numbers with no source.](/blog/slop-stats.webp)

The bar exists because a landing page has a stats bar, not because those four facts were needed.

![Three testimonials with truncated names and initial avatars.](/blog/slop-testimonials.webp)

Same logic in the testimonials: first names followed by an initial.

**What you should do:** a testimonial with a real name, a real role, and a real face.

## Two tools to stop shipping slop.

Your eye is enough to spot the eight signs. To stop producing them, you have to work on the machine, at both ends.

**Upstream: [Taste Skill](https://www.tasteskill.dev/).** Open source skill files your agent loads before it writes a single line. They force it to read the brief, pick a direction, then pass a checklist before shipping. One command: `npx skills add Leonxlnx/taste-skill`.

**Downstream: [Impeccable](https://impeccable.style/slop/).** A catalog of 46 patterns that mark an interface as AI-generated, plus a detector that highlights them in place on the page. The command: `npx impeccable detect`.
