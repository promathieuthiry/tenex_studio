Two weeks for a full site sounds short. It is not a shortcut. It is a constraint we accept because it forces the right decisions at the right time.

## The deadline is won before the first pixel.

Most projects slip upstream, not in production. We lock scope, content, and visual direction in the first week. Once those three are fixed, the rest moves fast.

- A clear scope. One page per goal, not one page per whim.
- Content first. We write before we design.
- A visual direction approved early, not at the finish line.
- Short decision points. No approvals left to drag.

## Craft does not get cut.

Holding the deadline does not mean shipping a template. Speed comes from process, not from lowering quality. The site stays custom, fast, and clean.

> Speed is a result of framing, not a sacrifice on quality.
