# SEO Strategy

*Source of truth: .agents/product-marketing.md*

*Last updated: 2026-06-14*

## Strategic Position

Tenex Studio should not chase generic agency traffic first. The strongest SEO position is expert-service websites: custom sites for firms where trust, taste, credentials and clarity decide whether a prospect books a call.

The core SEO promise:

> Custom websites for expert-service firms that need to prove trust before the first call.

The strategy should focus on commercial searches by vertical and by trust problem. Awareness content is useful later, but the first build should create pages that can convert high-intent professional-service buyers.

This follows Google Search Central's guidance that SEO should help search engines understand content and help users decide whether to visit, while content should be helpful, reliable and people-first.

## SEO Goals

1. Rank for premium custom website searches in expert-service categories.
2. Capture high-intent searches from law firms, accountants, consultants, architects, private clinics, wealth advisors, real estate professionals, recruiters, technical consultancies and B2B expert agencies.
3. Turn organic visits into booked calls, not newsletter subscribers.
4. Build enough topical authority to support future vertical pages, case studies and service content.
5. Keep FR and EN content paired, canonical and indexable.

## Technical SEO Priorities

1. Confirm every FR page has an EN counterpart and every EN page has an FR counterpart.
2. Add self-referencing canonicals for each locale page.
3. Add reciprocal `hreflang` links, including `fr`, `en` and `x-default`, using absolute canonical URLs.
4. Ensure sitemap includes all canonical indexable pages.
5. Keep static Astro output fast. The target should support strong Core Web Vitals.
6. Add structured data after page architecture is settled:
   - `Organization`.
   - `ProfessionalService` or `LocalBusiness`.
   - `WebSite`.
   - `FAQPage` where visible FAQ content exists.
   - `BreadcrumbList` for service, vertical and case study pages.
7. Keep title tags specific to intent, not brand-first on every page.
8. Keep meta descriptions written for booking intent.

## On-Page Template

Every commercial or vertical page should follow this structure:

1. H1 naming the service or vertical in plain language.
2. One paragraph that names the buyer, trust problem and business outcome.
3. Proof block with one relevant testimonial, case result or portfolio example.
4. Service or page structure section with what Tenex actually delivers.
5. Trust section covering credentials, cases, portfolio, team or patient/client reassurance.
6. Process section with timeline and decision points.
7. Differentiation section against templates, sector vendors, large agencies or DIY.
8. FAQ section answering cost, timeline, ownership, compliance-sensitive review and fit.
9. CTA to book a call.

## Internal Linking

Use a simple hub structure:

- Home links to core commercial pages.
- Core service pages link to vertical pages.
- Vertical pages link back to the relevant service page.
- Case studies link to the service and vertical they prove.
- Blog posts link to one service page and one relevant case study or vertical page.

Avoid orphan articles. Every article should support a commercial or vertical page.

## Measurement Plan

Set up or review:

- Google Search Console.
- Vercel Analytics and Speed Insights.
- Booking attribution for Cal.com clicks.
- Organic landing page reports.
- Index coverage for FR and EN pages.
- Queries by vertical, not just total impressions.

Primary SEO KPIs:

- Booked calls from organic traffic.
- Organic landing page conversion rate.
- Top 10 rankings for core commercial and vertical terms.
- Indexed pages by locale.
- Organic impressions for expert-service queries.

## 90-Day Execution Plan

### Days 1-15

- Run a technical SEO audit.
- Confirm sitemap, robots, canonicals and hreflang.
- Rewrite homepage SEO title and description around expert-service websites.
- Decide final URL architecture for service and vertical pages.
- Prepare Content Collections migration plan for blog and case studies.

### Days 16-45

- Publish or strengthen the five core commercial pages.
- Add structured data for organization, website, services and FAQ.
- Add internal links from home, services, pricing, FAQ and work sections.
- Build first two case studies: Studio Lumen and Renardo Tech.

### Days 46-75

- Publish the first four vertical pages: law firms, accountants, consultants and architects.
- Publish four supporting articles from Pillars 1 and 3.
- Add comparison content for template vs custom and sector specialist vs custom studio.

### Days 76-90

- Review Search Console query data.
- Improve pages with impressions but low click-through rate.
- Add FAQs based on real queries.
- Decide whether programmatic SEO is justified. Do it only for vertical patterns with real demand and unique content.

## What Not To Do

- Do not target founder-led companies, startups, fundraising or demo day language as the main ICP.
- Do not anchor premium buyers on the EUR 1,500 offer.
- Do not publish generic agency blog posts like "Why you need a website."
- Do not create thin vertical pages by swapping industry names.
- Do not create thin location pages for every city.
- Do not create FR pages without EN equivalents.
- Do not cross-canonicalize FR and EN pages.
- Do not chase traffic that has no path to a booked call.
