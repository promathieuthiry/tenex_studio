## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** The firm's expertise is strong, but the website does not make that expertise credible, clear or premium enough before a prospect reaches out.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Templates make expert firms look interchangeable.
- Generic agency copy does not capture nuance, credentials or seniority.
- Wix, Webflow and Framer rent a platform instead of delivering owned code.
- Large agencies add handoffs, slower cycles and diluted accountability.
- Internal teams often lack the time or editorial distance to clarify the offer.
- Sector-specific vendors can make every firm in the category look the same.

**What it costs them:** Poor-quality inquiries, lost trust, weaker referrals, lower perceived fees, unclear differentiation, fewer bookings and prospects choosing a competitor before making contact.

**Emotional tension:** The team knows the work is serious and valuable. The website feels behind the practice. It does not carry the same authority as the people, cases, portfolio or client outcomes behind it.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct:** Premium web agencies and digital studios serving professional services, medical practices, real estate, consulting and B2B services. They can fall short when the work is slow, generic or visually polished without a clear conversion structure.

**Secondary:** Sector-specific website vendors, template shops and no-code studios. They can fall short when the buyer needs a distinctive identity, owned code, editorial structure and premium positioning.

**Indirect:** In-house DIY, old referrals-only websites, local IT providers, brand-only studios and "we will fix the site later." They can fall short when reputation and qualification now depend on the site.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- Strong fit for expert-service firms where trust and taste decide whether a prospect calls.
- Custom identity and structure, not recycled templates.
- One accountable owner from first call to launch.
- Production frontend quality with lightweight web app capability.
- Code and content ownership for the client.
- Bilingual FR/EN delivery.
- Ability to make complex expertise clear without flattening it.

**How we do it differently:** TeneX combines positioning, copy, design and engineering in one focused production workflow. The work starts from the firm's expertise, proof and client decision path, then turns that into page structure, editorial hierarchy, design system and implementation.

**Why that's better:** The client gets a site that feels specific to the firm, is easier to trust and is built to qualify better inquiries. There is less coordination cost, less generic output and clearer ownership after launch.

**Why customers choose us:** They need a credible, premium site that shows expertise before a call. They want their website to match the quality of their work, attract better-fit clients and avoid the interchangeable look of template-based professional-service sites.

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Can I get something cheaper with a template?" | Yes, if the website is not affecting trust or qualification. TeneX is for expert firms whose site needs to justify premium fees before a call. |
| "Do you understand our regulated or technical field?" | The work starts with structure, proof and plain-language explanation. Specialist review can be added where legal, medical or financial precision is required. |
| "Can a small studio handle the full project?" | Yes. The studio is one accountable owner for design and engineering, with named specialists added only when needed. |
| "Will I be dependent after delivery?" | No. The code belongs to the client, content can be edited through a CMS when included, and handover is part of the work. |
| "Will the site still feel premium if it is delivered quickly?" | Yes, when scope is clear. Existing proof shows focused delivery in one day to under two weeks without relying on recycled templates. |

**Anti-persona:** Buyers who want "just something basic," do not value positioning or taste, need only a low-cost brochure, want to copy a competitor, compare only against template prices or do not care about attracting better-qualified clients.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push:** Current site looks generic, dated, hard to trust, hard to update, poorly structured or out of sync with the firm's level of expertise.

**Pull:** A custom site with stronger authority, clearer service positioning, better proof, sharper portfolio or case presentation, and client ownership.

**Habit:** Relying on referrals, keeping the old website because it is "good enough," patching a template or postponing the redesign until the next hiring, event or growth push.

**Anxiety:** Fear that custom work will be expensive, slow, subjective, hard to maintain or risky in a regulated field.
